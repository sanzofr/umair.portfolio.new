Currently reading

What I'm reading — and why it's changing how I think.

I'm currently deep into The Uncommon Sense of Marketing by Sanjay Tiwari, and its core message is one every marketer needs to hear:

"Get the basics right — and everything else follows."

But the insights go far deeper than "basics." Tiwari unpacks one of the most important distinctions in all of marketing: the difference between a need and a want.

That gap between need and want is where all great marketing lives. The brands that win aren't just visible — they're inevitable. They've already positioned themselves so precisely in the consumer's mind that when the need appears, their name surfaces automatically.

For me, this isn't just theory. It's a lens I now apply to every campaign I run, every piece of content I plan, and every brand I study. Reading this has changed how I look at every ad I see, every brand I work with, and every strategy I build.
